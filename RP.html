Project Overview

This research project investigates how Vietnam Airlines' CSR activities influence customer retention, satisfaction, and loyalty. The study aims to:

  • Identify the key dimensions of Vietnam Airlines' CSR practices that impact customer perception and loyalty.
  • Evaluate the mediating role of corporate image in the relationship between CSR practices and customer loyalty.
  • Provide actionable recommendations for Vietnam Airlines to enhance its CSR strategies to foster customer loyalty in the postâ€“COVID-19 era.

Tools & Methodology

Research Design
  • Positivist philosophy and deductive approach
  • Mono-method quantitative study using a cross-sectional survey
Data Collection
  • Online structured questionnaire distributed via social media and email
  • Available in Vietnamese and English
  • Convenience sampling, resulting in 316 valid responses
Analysis Techniques
  • Descriptive statistics for demographic and response data
  • Reliability Analysis: Cronbach's alpha for internal consistency
  • Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA): To extract and validate CSR dimensions
  • Structural Equation Modeling (SEM): To test relationships
  • Multiple Linear Regression & One-way ANOVA
  • Analytical tools: Microsoft Excel, SPSS, AMOS

Key Findings & Insights

CSR Dimensions

Five primary CSR dimensions were confirmed: Economic, Legal, Ethical, Philanthropic, and Environmental.

Strong Predictors

Economic, Philanthropic, and Ethical CSR activities have significant positive impacts on both corporate image and customer loyalty, with Philanthropic CSR being the strongest predictor for corporate image.

Corporate Image & Segment Insights

Corporate image mediates the effect of CSR on customer loyalty and is essential in translating CSR activities into customer retention. There are statistically significant differences in CSR perceptions and loyalty across age groups, suggesting the need for targeted CSR communication strategies.
