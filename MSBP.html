Project Overview & Objectives

This project investigated how the COVID-19 pandemic affected the retail industry in Vietnam and analyzed the strategies and initiatives retail businesses adopted for recovery. The core objectives were:

  • Analyze the impact of COVID-19 on retail sales, consumer behavior, and supply chains.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of business recovery strategies implemented by retail businesses post-pandemic.
  • Propose actionable recommendations for retailers to ensure long-term sustainability and resilience in a post-pandemic environment.

Tools & Methodology

Primary & Secondary Research
  • Quantitative online survey (Google Forms), 103 respondents
  • Secondary research: academic literature and industry reports
Analysis & Management
  • Excel for descriptive statistics and visualizations
  • WBS and Gantt charts for planning
Collaboration & Communication
  • Microsoft Teams, email, and messaging platforms

Key Findings & Insights

Sales & Supply Chains

Sales showed moderate declines; ~37% perceived significant supply chain disruptions with segment-specific variation.

Behavior & Strategies

Shift toward health/home items and partial online adoption. Effective strategies: e-commerce expansion, promotions, digital marketing, and in-store safety.

Digital & Collaboration

Few invested heavily in digital transformation or partnerships, indicating focus on short-term survival over long-term ecosystem solutions.
